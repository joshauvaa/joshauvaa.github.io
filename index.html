// about me

Always Learning.

I've worn a lot of different hats. At the University of Washington, I studied Informatics with a focus in Human-Computer Interaction. My coursework balanced development and design, always in service of building things that actually help people.

That mindset followed me everywhere after. As a Student IT Consultant, it meant troubleshooting for technical and non-technical users alike, never losing sight of the person behind the ticket. As a marketing intern, it meant auditing brand recognizability and reputability to make sure the people on the other end of an ML-driven model could actually trust what they were seeing. Now, in customer service, the recurring pain points I hear have me thinking about where the experience could improve.

Throughout all of my different roles, one instinct remains the same: center the user.
