Context

Cascade Bicycle Club is a real Seattle-based nonprofit focused on cycling advocacy, education, and community events across the Pacific Northwest. Our team was tasked with designing what would become Radian: a complete design system and component library for Cascade's digital presence, built around four guiding principles: Intuitive, Unified, Universal, and Iterate.

The need for a system was supported by an interface audit our team ran on Cascade's existing site. Below are the actual notes from that audit.

The Audience

Personas

Tom and June were synthesized from the client brief, our interface audit, and secondary research to represent experienced riders and newcomers. While not a substitute for primary interviews, they gave the team a shared reference point for evaluating design decisions.

Tom
40 · 2nd-year Cascade member · Confident rider
Pain Points
  • Outdated/worn-out biking equipment
  • Hard to assist new members navigating the platform
Needs
  • An easy-to-navigate platform so he can help others find events and resources
June
35 · New to cycling · Mother of two, new to the PNW
Pain Points
  • No experienced background in cycling
  • Safety concerns; no idea where to find family-friendly information

Takeaways · Problem · Goal

Big Takeaways

Combining the audit and the persona work, the takeaway was that Cascade's site didn't have a design problem in any one place, but rather a systems problem. Individual pages and components were decently designed in isolation, but they lacked cohesion as a visual whole.

The Problem

Cascade Bicycle Club's digital presence lacks a unified visual language. Typography, color, and component styles vary inconsistently across pages, making the experience feel fragmented and difficult to navigate for both confident members like Tom and newcomers like June alike.

The Goal

Design a full system composing of colors, type, components, and patterns that any page on Cascade's site could be built from.

Testing · Final System

User Testing & Feedback

Informal feedback from classmates and peers helped refine navigation patterns, component behavior, and form layouts before the final presentation. While not a formal usability study with Cascade members, it improved the consistency of the system before handoff.

The Design System

Below are core components of the design system.

Color System
Primary, secondary, and neutral tokens — named and documented with exact hex values, built around Cascade's brand green.
Typography
Full h1–h5 scale in Raleway — confident enough for headers, still legible for dense content like event descriptions.
Buttons, Nav & Input
Every button built with real interaction states (enabled, hovered, icon variant), a full branded nav bar, and labeled sign-in and account creation forms.
Cards
Activity cards in multiple sizes — the direct product of designing for June, not just Tom: clear hierarchy, explicit labels, no icon-only shorthand.
Patterns
Components composed into real, reusable page layouts — proof the library holds up at the page level, not just as isolated pieces.

Prototypes

The system was built out into working prototypes for both desktop and mobile, confirming the component library held up responsively rather than just looking good as static documentation.

Closing

Radian is the most complete visual artifact in my portfolio.
